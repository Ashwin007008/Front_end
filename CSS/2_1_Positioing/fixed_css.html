Types of images Digital images Digital images are stored as data, often from digital cameras or computer graphics. They are mainly of two types: Raster (or Bitmap) images: Composed of a grid of pixels. Their resolution depends on the number of pixels, and excessive enlargement can cause pixelation. Examples include JPEG, PNG, and GIF. Vector images: Defined by mathematical equations representing shapes, lines, and curves. They can be scaled without quality loss and are used for logos, icons, and illustrations. Analog images These are physical visual representations on materials like paper or canvas. Film photographs are created by light on a photosensitive surface. Optical images In optics, an image is a reproduction of an object formed by light waves, such as a reflection.
